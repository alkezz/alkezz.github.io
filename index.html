About me

Hi nice to see you!
My name is Ali Ezzeddine and I am a Full Stack Software Engineer with a passion for problem-solving and creativity!

As a graduate who made his way through App Academy's rigorous course, I have increased my knowledge in programming and enjoyed using languages such as JavaScript and Python, while quenching my thirst for creativity with React and HTML/CSS. Currently, I am self-learning C# and angular JS

Through my journey, I've created three full-stack projects, each focusing on a different part of the industry. My Spotify project (named Bopify) takes inspiration from Spotify's design as well as the features that Spotify offers. As a huge music lover, being able to recreate something I use everyday was always my goal and I'm glad I'm almost there!

When I'm not coding, you can find me reading a good book (if you haven't read Slaughterhouse-Five by Kurt Vonnegut you're missing out!), hiking, going to art museums (cover art was painted by Tomas Cole), and of course gaming. I love to unwind playing story rich games such as Metal Gear Solid V, Death Stranding, or Red Dead Redemption 2. If I want to turn my brain off, games like Stardew Valley will do the job.

Projects

  • All
  1. Bopify - A Spotify Clone

    Bopify is a music streaming service that takes inspiration from Spotify.
    Built using a Python/Flask back end and a React/Redux front end

  2. Enhance

    Enhance is a portfolio sharing website where users can upload art they created to share with other users and make connections.
    Built using a Python/Flask back end and a React/Redux front end

  3. GroundBnb

    GroundBnb was my first full stack project. Like AirBnb, it allows users to see properties that other users have put up for rent.
    Unlike Airbnb, it's primarly focused on caves around the world. Customers can book time for a cave, leave reviews that average out the star rating for each cave, and create their own cave listing!
    Built using a JavaScript/Express back end with a React/Redux front end. Sequelize was used for the local database.
